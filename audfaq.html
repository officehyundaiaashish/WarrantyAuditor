Getting Started
What is this app used for?
Audit-it is a professional dealership tool for auditing warranty claims. It lets you import Excel/CSV claim files, audit each claim for discrepancies, tag parts as Clean, Unclear, or Pending, then export finalized PDF or Excel reports — all with AES-256 encryption and cloud backup.
How do I start a new audit?
Go to Main Menu → New Audit. Set the From and To audit period (month/year), then upload your Excel or CSV claim file. The app will detect columns automatically and load your claims into the workspace.
  • The audit period determines which month's data is expected in the file.
  • If the file's dates don't match the period you set, the app will warn you.
What file format is supported for import?
Supported formats: .xlsx .xls .csv

The app auto-detects key columns: Claim No, Part Description, Amount, Claim Type, Date. If a required column like Claim No is missing, you'll see an error. Make sure column headers are on the first row.
How do I log in?
The app uses Google Sign-In via Firebase. Click Sign in with Google on the login screen and choose your authorised account. Your session is restored automatically — no need to sign in every time.
  • Only accounts registered by your admin can access the app.
  • If your account is blocked, contact your administrator.
Why does it ask for a verification code (TOTP)?
The app uses Two-Factor Authentication (TOTP) for extra security. After signing in with Google you will be asked for a 6-digit code from your Authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator). This session is valid for 3 hours — after that, you'll be asked again.
  • If the app keeps asking for TOTP, try refreshing the page once.
  • Make sure your device clock is accurate — TOTP codes are time-sensitive.
Auditing Claims
How do I audit a single claim?
In the workspace, tap any claim row to open the audit modal. Select the Discrepancy Status from the dropdown, add an optional remark, then tap Save Entry.
  • The row colour updates instantly: Green = Clean, Amber = Unclear, Grey = Pending.
  • You can re-open any audited claim to change its status at any time while the audit is a Draft.
What do Clean, Unclear, and Pending mean?
Clean (Cln) — Part audited, no discrepancy found. Claim is fine.

Unclear (Unc) — A discrepancy was found (e.g. part missing, batch mismatch, not tagged). Needs follow-up.

Pending (Pen) — Claim has not been audited yet. No entry saved for this claim.
What are the default discrepancy codes?
The built-in discrepancy codes are:
  • 0 — None Found Clean
  • 1 — Part is clean (Sent HMIL Suspense) Clean
  • 3 — Part is Missing Unclear
  • 4 — Part Batch Code Mismatch Unclear
  • 5 — Part Not Tagged Unclear
  • 6 — Part Packing Missing Unclear
  • 7 — Related Part Missing / Affected Part Missing Unclear
  • 8 — Part is Not Defective Unclear
  • 9 — Part is Damaged Unclear
  • 10 — Issue part casual issue. Else Fine [CLN]
  • 11 — Labour Only Claim [CLN]
You can add your own codes via Main Menu → Custom Codes.
Why are some claims not showing in my audit?
The app automatically filters out certain claim types before loading:
  • Free Service / FSC claims — excluded always.
  • Campaign claims under ₹300 — excluded unless the part description contains Power Window or Fuel Pump.
This is by design so only auditable warranty claims appear in your workspace.
What is Suspense(P) and how do I handle it?
Suspense(P) means a part was tagged as Sent to HMIL Suspense (Code 1) but the physical dispatch to the Technical Centre has not been confirmed yet.

To resolve it, open the claim, go to the Suspense section in the modal, and tap Sent once the part is dispatched. You can optionally enter a POD number.

A red Suspense(P) — Action Required banner will appear on the main menu until all pending suspense parts are marked as sent.
Can I add notes or remarks to a claim?
Yes. Every claim modal has an Optional Remark field at the bottom. Type any note (e.g. "Part found in bin 3", "Will follow up Monday") and it will be saved with that claim entry and included in exported reports.
Finalising & Reports
What happens when I finalise an audit?
Finalising locks the audit and moves it to Previous Audits (Closed). After finalising:
  • The audit gets an Audit Number (e.g. WA-2025-001).
  • No further edits can be made to claim entries.
  • The audit appears in the Dashboard and Reports Export.
  • Data is saved securely in local storage (AES-256 encrypted if security is on).
What is an Audit Number and how is it generated?
An Audit Number is a unique reference assigned when you finalise an audit (e.g. WA-2025-003). It is auto-incremented based on previous audits.

In the Archive screen you can also manually generate or cancel an audit number for a closed audit using the action buttons on each row.
How do I export a report?
Go to Main Menu → Reports Export. Choose your scope (Master / specific month) and filter (All Claims, Clear Only, Unclear Only), then tap Export Excel or Export PDF.

Reports include: Claim No, Part Description, Amount, Discrepancy Status, and Remarks — with totals at the bottom.
How do I discard or delete a draft audit?
Inside the active workspace, tap the ⋮ menu (top-right) and select Discard Workspace. This permanently deletes the draft and all unsaved progress.

Alternatively, go to Drafts in the Archive and delete it from there. This action cannot be undone.
Can I refresh or update the claim file mid-audit?
Yes. In the workspace header, tap the ↻ Refresh (re-upload) button to upload a new version of the claim file for the same audit period. The app will validate the file dates match the audit period before replacing the data. Your existing audit entries are preserved where claim numbers match.
Dashboard
What does the Dashboard show?
The Dashboard gives a summary view of all claims across one or all audits:
  • Clean — Total claims with no discrepancy.
  • Unclear — Claims with a discrepancy tagged.
  • Pending — Claims not yet audited.
It also shows a doughnut chart with percentages, claim amount breakdowns, and lets you filter the table by status.
What is Safe Zone vs Danger Zone in analytics?
The analytics notification on the main menu shows your audit health:
  • ✅ Safe Zone — Clean ≥ 70% and Unclear ≤ 15%.
  • ⚠️ Danger Zone — Clean < 70% or Unclear > 15%.
This gives a quick health indicator of your dealership's warranty audit performance across all finalised months.
What is the "Action Required" notification on the menu?
This red banner appears when one or more finalised audits still have Unclear or Pending claims. Click it to go directly to the Dashboard filtered to the affected month(s). The banner stays until all claims are resolved — it cannot be dismissed while issues remain.
Backup & Restore
What backup options are available?
There are three backup methods under Backup & Restore:
  • Local — Download a full .json backup file to your device. Best for manual offline backup.
  • Firebase — Sign in with Google to sync data to Firebase Firestore cloud. Ideal for multi-device access.
  • GitHub — Connect via a Personal Access Token to sync to a private GitHub Gist. Can be set to auto-sync on every audit close.
How do I do a local backup and restore?
Backup: Go to Backup & Restore → Local tab and tap Download Full Backup. A .json file is saved to your device.

Restore: On the same screen, tap Browse File and select your .json backup. Warning: this overwrites all current data.
How do I back up to Firebase / use on a new device?
Go to Backup & Restore → Firebase tab and tap Sign in with Google. Once connected:
  • Tap Backup to Firebase to push your data to the cloud.
  • On a new device, sign in, then tap Restore from Firebase to pull it back.
The cloud status card shows when the last backup was made.
How do I set up GitHub Gist backup and auto-sync?
Go to Backup & Restore → GitHub tab. Paste your GitHub Personal Access Token (needs gist scope) and tap Connect. Once connected:
  • Use Backup to Gist / Restore from Gist manually.
  • Enable Auto-Sync to automatically push to Gist every time you close/finalise an audit.
Your token is stored AES-256 encrypted locally — never sent to any server except GitHub.
What are the quick backup/restore buttons in the header?
The header has two shortcut icons (cloud upload and cloud download) for fast one-tap backup and restore using your connected cloud service (Firebase or GitHub Gist). You can also access them from the ⋮ user menu → Backup to Cloud / Restore from Cloud.
Security
What is the App Launch Lock?
When enabled, the app requires a Master Password every time it is opened. Go to Security Settings and toggle App Launch Lock on. You will be prompted to set a Master Password on first enable.

This is separate from your Google login — it is a local PIN/password to prevent unauthorised access on a shared device.
What is Archive Protection?
When Archive Protection is enabled, opening or deleting a Closed (finalised) audit requires the Master Password. This prevents accidental deletion or unauthorised viewing of completed audit records.
Is my audit data encrypted?
Yes. When security is active, all audit data stored locally is encrypted with AES-256. Data is stored in your browser's IndexedDB (via localforage) — it never leaves your device unless you explicitly use a cloud backup feature.
User Management
How do I add a new user?
Only an Admin can add users. Go to User Management (visible to admins only) and fill in the user's Name, Email, Dealer ID, set their Role (Admin/Viewer), and choose which screens they can access. Tap Add User to register them in Firestore.

The new user can then sign in with their Google account matching that email.
How do I block a user?
In User Management, find the user's card and tap Block. A blocked user will see an error when they try to log in and will be redirected immediately. Their data in Firestore is not deleted — you can unblock them at any time.
What screens can each role access?
When adding or editing a user, you can individually toggle access to:
  • New Audit  ·  Drafts & Archives  ·  Dashboard
  • Reports Export  ·  Backup & Restore
  • Custom Codes  ·  Security Settings
Admin accounts have all screens enabled by default. Viewer accounts only get New Audit and Drafts & Archives by default.
Can an admin reset a user's password?
Since login is via Google Sign-In, passwords are managed by Google — not by this app. Admins can however force-change the Master Password for local security locks via the admin panel in User Management (if enabled).
Custom Codes
How do I add my own discrepancy code?
Go to Main Menu → Custom Codes. Enter a unique Code Number and a Description, then choose whether it marks a claim as Unclear or Clean. Tap + Add Code.

Your custom code will immediately appear in the discrepancy dropdown inside the audit modal.
Can I delete a custom code?
Yes. In the Custom Codes screen, tap the Delete button next to any code in the table. Custom codes can be deleted freely. Default system codes (0–11) cannot be deleted — only custom ones you've added appear in the table.
Troubleshooting
My data seems to have disappeared — what happened?
All data is stored in your browser's IndexedDB. It can be lost if you:
  • Cleared browser data / site data / cookies.
  • Used a different browser or private/incognito mode.
  • Switched devices without restoring a backup first.
Always use Backup & Restore regularly. If you had Firebase or GitHub Gist connected, try Restore from Cloud to recover your data.
I get a "Wrong File" error when uploading — why?
The app checks that the dates in your claim file match the audit period you set (From/To months). If your file contains claims from a different month, you'll see a Wrong File! alert showing the mismatch.

Fix: either correct the audit period dates, or upload the correct file for that period.
The app can't detect my file columns — what should I check?
The app looks for standard column names in the first row of your sheet. Make sure your file has recognisable headers such as:
  • Claim No (or Claim Number, Claim ID)
  • Part Description (or Part Desc, Description)
  • Amount (or Claim Amount, Total)
  • Claim Type (or Type, Category)
If your headers are in a merged cell or a row other than row 1, the detection will fail. Try restructuring your file so headers are in row 1.
The app keeps asking for TOTP every time I open it.
The TOTP session lasts 3 hours. If it's expiring too quickly:
  • Check your device clock — TOTP is time-based; a wrong clock causes instant expiry.
  • Make sure you're not using private/incognito mode (session storage is cleared on close).
  • If you're on a shared device and multiple people log in, each user has their own session.
If the problem persists, try hard-refreshing the page (Ctrl+Shift+R).
After clicking a notification, the Dashboard dropdown looks empty.
The dropdown shows the selected month in blue bold text with a blue border when active. If it still looks grey/empty, try tapping the dropdown once to open and close it — this refreshes the visual state. The data will still be loaded correctly even if the visual appears blank.
Tips & Shortcuts
How do I switch between Dark Mode and Light Mode?
Tap the sun / moon icon in the top-right corner of the header bar. The theme switches instantly between Light and Dark mode. Your preference is saved automatically and restored every time you open the app.
How do I see all keyboard shortcuts?
Press ? on your keyboard at any time to open the Keyboard Shortcuts overlay. Key shortcuts include:
  • Esc — Go back / close modal / exit workspace
  • Enter — Open first claim / save audit entry
  • ← → — Previous / Next page in workspace or dashboard
  • 0–9 / A–Z — Type to instantly search claims
  • ↑ ↓ — Navigate dropdown options
  • 0–9 — Quick-select discrepancy code inside audit modal
What is the keyboard icon next to number inputs?
Some number input fields have a small keyboard icon button on the right side. Tapping it toggles between:
  • Numeric-only mode — Shows a numeric keypad on mobile; restricts input to numbers only.
  • Full keyboard mode — Allows typing letters and symbols (useful for TV remotes or barcode scanners).
This is especially useful on TV or large-screen setups where a full keyboard is not available.
How does pagination work in the workspace and dashboard?
Claims are shown in pages to keep the interface fast. Use the ← Prev and Next → buttons at the bottom of the table to move between pages. You can also press the ← → arrow keys on your keyboard to navigate pages quickly.
How do I quickly find a specific claim in the workspace?
Use the Search bar at the top of the workspace to filter by Claim No or Part Description. You can also simply start typing (any letter or number) and the search bar activates automatically — no need to click it first. The table updates in real time as you type.
